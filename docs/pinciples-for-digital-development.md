# IDS-DRR: Adherence to Principles for Digital Development

This document maps the IDS-DRR platform — [Frontend](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend), [Data Management](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management), and [risk-score-model-generic](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/risk-score-model-generic) — to the nine [Principles for Digital Development](https://digitalprinciples.org) (2024).

| # | Principle | Evidence |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **Understand the Existing Ecosystem** | Built in response to fragmented disaster data across Indian SDMAs, IMD, NIC, and district administrations; integrates existing sources (NIC ArcGIS boundaries, Bhuvan flood inundation, WorldPop, NASADEM, IMD rainfall, SDMA losses/damages, GePNIC e-procurement) rather than parallel infrastructure. Piloted with [ASDMA in Assam](https://assamtribune.com/assam/assam-unveils-ids-drr-platform-to-boost-disaster-risk-reduction-with-geospatial-tech-1569494), replicated in Odisha, UP, Bihar, and Himachal Pradesh. |
| 2 | **Share, Reuse, and Improve** | All repos openly licensed — MIT ([Frontend](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/dev/LICENSE)), AGPL-3.0 ([Data Management](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management/blob/dev/LICENSE), [Risk Model](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/risk-score-model-generic/blob/main/LICENSE), configurable beyond the Indian context). Citable via [CITATION.cff](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/risk-score-model-generic/blob/main/CITATION.cff); reused independently by an NYU Capstone research team. |
| 3 | **Design with People** | Co-developed with ASDMA and field practitioners. The live platform ([drr.open-contracting.in](https://drr.open-contracting.in/en)) is open-access and built for government officials and community stakeholders, not just data scientists, with [i18n](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/tree/main/i18n)/[locales](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/tree/main/locales) support for non-English-speaking users. |
| 4 | **Design for Inclusion** | Accessible Frontend (semantic HTML, screen-reader-friendly); multi-language support via [i18n](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/tree/main/i18n); open access with no login, so flood risk data reaches vulnerable communities, officials, and journalists alike. |
| 5 | **Build for Sustainability** | *Technical:* Dockerised ([Frontend](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/main/Dockerfile), [Data Management](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management/blob/main/Dockerfile)), decoupled branding ([ids-drr-india-branding](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/ids-drr-india-branding)), pinned dependencies, tagged [v0.1.0](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management/releases/tag/v0.1.0) release. *Institutional:* hosted under the [Open Contracting Partnership](https://www.open-contracting.org/) ecosystem at `drr.open-contracting.in`; adopted and publicly launched by ASDMA in Assam, then replicated with state authorities in Odisha, UP, Bihar, and Himachal Pradesh, with lessons feeding back into a shared core platform. |
| 6 | **Establish People-First Data Practices** | Operates on aggregated administrative-level data (revenue circle/district), not individual records. Sources are open government datasets (IMD, NIC, Census, NASADEM) and public procurement data. AGPL-3.0 on the data-management layer requires derivative deployments to stay open. |
| 7 | **Create Open and Transparent Practices** | Full public commit history and open issue tracking ([Frontend](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/issues), [Data Management](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management/issues)). Open standards throughout: GraphQL, [GeoJSON](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/tree/main/geo_json), [JSON config](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Data-Management/blob/main/report_config.example.json), PostGIS. Methodology is documented and citable via [CITATION.cff](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/risk-score-model-generic/blob/main/CITATION.cff). |
| 8 | **Anticipate and Mitigate Harms** | Sentry error monitoring across client, edge, and server ([client](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/main/sentry.client.config.ts)/[edge](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/main/sentry.edge.config.ts)/[server](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/main/sentry.server.config.ts) configs); [`env.sample`](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/IDS-DRR-Frontend/blob/main/env.sample) prevents credential leaks; TypeScript catches data-shape errors at compile time; 60% test coverage via Jest guards against regressions that could mislead disaster-response decisions. |
| 9 | **Use Evidence to Improve Outcomes** | Combines hazard, exposure, vulnerability, and government-response indicators, shaped by structured consultation with ASDMA (Assam pilot) and state authorities in Odisha, UP, Bihar, and Himachal Pradesh to validate indicators and surface data gaps. The [risk model](https://github.com/CivicDataLab/risk-score-model-generic) is configurable to locally validated indicators rather than a fixed formula; cross-state deployment enables comparative learning; an independent NYU Capstone analysis provides external validation; |
